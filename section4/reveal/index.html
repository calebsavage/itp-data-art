Social Insecurity:

What's In a Number?

Background

  • Social Security was created during the great depression. It provides benefits to retirees and people with disabilities.
  • American workers pay into the program with payroll taxes their entire careers, and receive benefits once they reach retirement age.
  • When you think about it, the system is not entirely unlike a ponzi scheme... but that's a topic for a different discussion.

Administration

  • The Social Security Administration is a federal agency which administers the program.
  • This effort requires a massive volume of data to be collected, stored, and analyzed over a period of many decades.
  • This sort of high-volume data collection and processing was enabled by the advent of tabulating machines.
  • It all starts with a number...

The Era of Electromechanical Data Processing

The First Age of Big Data

  • Herman Hollierith's tabulating devices were developed in the 1880s in response to an urgent need to automate the accounting of the 1890 census-- tallying the results from 1880's census took eight years.
  • The earliest examples could do basic counting and sorting operations with data supplied on punched cards.
  • By the mid-1930's, these tabulators could perform simple mathematical operations and work with alphanumeric data.
  • Before the advent of this technology, data processing was bottlenecked by the speed and accuracy of human "computers".
  • Without a means of automatic storage, sorting, and retrieval of information, there was necessarialy more importance placed on personal knowledge and human memory for recordkeeping.

Serial Number

  • Since 2011, number assignments have been randomized and the geographic and group numbers are no longer significant on newly-issued numbers.
  • There is no algorithmic way of determining the validity of a number. Other numbering schemes accomplish this with a "check digit" but the SSN scheme predates such conveniences and safeguards.

Cradle to Grave

  • Originally, a person would apply for a SSN when they began working.
  • Today, most parents apply for one on the same form as a birth certificate.
  • This immutable number sticks with a person through their entire life cycle, inscribed on nearly every official form from infancy through schooling, careers, medical and financial systems, and death.

Seven Million Missing Children

When Congress enacted laws requiring the SSN of children claimed as dependents on tax returns, the number of dependent children reported to the IRS dropped by 7 million the next year. This type of identity verification and fraud reduction has become the most common use of the SSN, overshadowing its purpose only as an account number in the Social Security Administration.

The Mainframe

  • Mainframe computing is a vastly different paradigm than today's standards.
  • Despite its "obsolescence," mainframe software technology dating back to the 1960s and 70s is still in extremely widespread use.
  • COBOL-- a programming language largely based on work by Admiral Grace Hopper-- originated in 1959.
  • Today, the SSA maintains ~60 million lines of mainframe COBOL code.
  • Despite massive efforts to modernize, it will still be many years before the SSA is free of this decased-old technology.
  • Programmer recruitment is a challenge, and businesses and agencies reliant on ancient code must often coax programmers out of retirement
  • "If it ain't broke..."

The Lifespan of a System

  • We expect our physical infrastructure like buildings and bridges to last for centuries with regular maintenance.
  • Why is 50 years seemingly ancient for a data system?
  • How can good business practices be used to maintain institutional knowledge-- what if the only guy who knows how the whole thing works gets hit by a bus?

       ELSE IF BF-BEGIN AND NOT DONE THEN
           PERFORM DO-BEGIN
           MOVE 99 TO LOOP-STATE
       ELSE IF BF-END AND NOT DONE THEN
           PERFORM DO-END
           MOVE 99 TO LOOP-STATE
       ELSE IF BF-DIE THEN
           GO TO HELL.
       ADD 1 TO CYCLES.
   DO-LEFT.
       IF CURRENT-CELL IS GREATER THAN 1 THEN
           SET CURRENT-CELL DOWN BY 1
  D        SUBTRACT 1 FROM DEBUG-CURRENT-CELL
       ELSE
  D        MOVE 9999 TO DEBUG-CURRENT-CELL
           SET CURRENT-CELL TO 9999.
       MOVE 99 TO LOOP-STATE.
   DO-RIGHT.
       IF CURRENT-CELL IS LESS THAN 9999 THEN
           SET CURRENT-CELL UP BY 1
  D        ADD 1 TO DEBUG-CURRENT-CELL
       ELSE
  D        MOVE 1 TO DEBUG-CURRENT-CELL
           SET CURRENT-CELL TO 1.
       MOVE 99 TO LOOP-STATE.
   DO-DEC.
       IF BF-CELL(CURRENT-CELL) > 0 THEN
           SUBTRACT 1 FROM BF-CELL(CURRENT-CELL)
       ELSE
           MOVE 255 TO BF-CELL(CURRENT-CELL).
       MOVE 99 TO LOOP-STATE.
   DO-INC.
       IF BF-CELL(CURRENT-CELL) < 255 THEN
           ADD 1 TO BF-CELL(CURRENT-CELL)
       ELSE
           MOVE 0 TO BF-CELL(CURRENT-CELL).
       MOVE 99 TO LOOP-STATE.
   DO-OUT.
       IF OUT-PTR < 999 THEN
           MOVE BF-CELL(CURRENT-CELL) TO CHAR-CODE
           CALL "DECODE-ASCII" USING ASCII-CHARACTER
           IF COBOL-STRING IS EQUAL TO SPACES THEN
               ADD 1 TO OUT-PTR
           ELSE
               STRING COBOL-STRING,
                      DELIMITED BY SPACES,
                      INTO BF-OUTPUT,
                      WITH POINTER OUT-PTR
  D            DISPLAY "OUT", I-O-CHARACTER, BF-CELL(CURRENT-CELL)
       ELSE
           GO TO HELL.
       MOVE 99 TO LOOP-STATE.
   DO-IN.
       UNSTRING BF-INPUT,
                INTO I-O-CHARACTER,
                WITH POINTER IN-PTR.
       IF NOT ESCAPE-CHAR THEN
           MOVE I-O-CHARACTER TO COBOL-STRING
       ELSE IF IN-PTR < 999 THEN
           UNSTRING BF-INPUT,
                    INTO I-O-CHARACTER,
                    WITH POINTER IN-PTR
           IF ESCAPE-CHAR THEN
               MOVE I-O-CHARACTER TO COBOL-STRING
           ELSE IF IN-PTR < 996 THEN
               SUBTRACT 1 FROM IN-PTR
               UNSTRING BF-INPUT,
                        DELIMITED BY "$",
                        INTO COBOL-STRING,
                        WITH POINTER IN-PTR
           ELSE GO TO HELL
       ELSE GO TO HELL.
       CALL "ENCODE-ASCII" USING ASCII-CHARACTER.
       MOVE CHAR-CODE TO BF-CELL(CURRENT-CELL).
  D    DISPLAY "IN", I-O-CHARACTER, BF-CELL(CURRENT-CELL).
       MOVE 99 TO LOOP-STATE.
   DO-BEGIN.
       IF BF-CELL(CURRENT-CELL) IS EQUAL TO 0 THEN
           MOVE LOOP-DEPTH TO LOOP-WORK
           ADD 1 TO LOOP-WORK
           PERFORM FIND-END UNTIL DONE
       ELSE
           ADD 1 TO LOOP-DEPTH.
       MOVE 99 TO LOOP-STATE.
       
    FIND-END.
  D    DISPLAY "ENTERED FIND-END."
       PERFORM READ-INSTRUCTION.
       IF NOT DONE THEN
           IF BF-BEGIN THEN
               ADD 1 TO LOOP-WORK
           ELSE IF BF-END THEN
  D            DISPLAY "FOUND END."
               SUBTRACT 1 FROM LOOP-WORK
               IF LOOP-WORK IS EQUAL TO LOOP-DEPTH THEN
                   MOVE 99 TO LOOP-STATE.
  D    DISPLAY "FIND-END.", IP, ";", CURRENT-INSTRUCTION,
  D            "LD", LOOP-DEPTH, "LW", LOOP-WORK.
       
   DO-END.
       SUBTRACT 1 FROM LOOP-DEPTH.
       IF BF-CELL(CURRENT-CELL) IS NOT EQUAL TO 0 THEN
           MOVE LOOP-DEPTH TO LOOP-WORK
           SUBTRACT 1 FROM IP
           PERFORM FIND-BEGIN UNTIL DONE.
       MOVE 99 TO LOOP-STATE.
   FIND-BEGIN.
  D    DISPLAY "ENTERED FIND-BEGIN."
       PERFORM UNREAD-INSTRUCTION.
       IF NOT DONE THEN
           IF BF-END THEN
               ADD 1 TO LOOP-WORK
           ELSE IF BF-BEGIN THEN
  D            DISPLAY "FOUND BEGIN."
               SUBTRACT 1 FROM LOOP-WORK
               IF LOOP-WORK IS EQUAL TO LOOP-DEPTH THEN
                   ADD 1 TO IP
                   MOVE 99 TO LOOP-STATE.
  D    DISPLAY "FIND-BEGIN.", IP, ";", CURRENT-INSTRUCTION,
  D            "LD", LOOP-DEPTH, "LW", LOOP-WORK.
   UNREAD-INSTRUCTION.
       UNSTRING BF-CODE INTO CURRENT-INSTRUCTION WITH POINTER IP.
       IF BF-ESCAPE THEN
  D        DISPLAY "PROCESSING ESCAPE"
           SUBTRACT 2 FROM IP
           PERFORM FIND-ESCAPE-BEGIN UNTIL DONE
           MOVE 0 TO LOOP-STATE
           ADD 2 TO IP
           UNSTRING BF-CODE DELIMITED BY "$"
                    INTO COBOL-STRING
                    WITH POINTER IP
  D        DISPLAY "ESCAPED CHARACTER. $", COBOL-STRING, "$"
           SUBTRACT 2 FROM IP
           PERFORM FIND-ESCAPE-BEGIN UNTIL DONE
           MOVE 0 TO LOOP-STATE
           IF COBOL-STRING IS EQUAL TO SPACES THEN
               MOVE "$" TO CURRENT-INSTRUCTION
           ELSE IF LSQB THEN
               MOVE "(" TO CURRENT-INSTRUCTION
           ELSE IF RSQB THEN
               MOVE ")" TO CURRENT-INSTRUCTION
           ELSE
               MOVE SPACE TO CURRENT-INSTRUCTION
       ELSE
  D        DISPLAY "NOT AN ESCAPE"
           IF IP IS GREATER THAN 2 THEN
               SUBTRACT 2 FROM IP
           ELSE
               MOVE 1 TO IP
           MOVE 99 TO LOOP-STATE.
   FIND-ESCAPE-BEGIN.
       UNSTRING BF-CODE INTO CURRENT-INSTRUCTION WITH POINTER IP.
  D    DISPLAY "PROCESSING CHARACTER. ", CURRENT-INSTRUCTION.
       IF BF-ESCAPE THEN
           MOVE 99 TO LOOP-STATE.
       IF IP IS GREATER THAN 2 THEN
           SUBTRACT 2 FROM IP
       ELSE
           MOVE 1 TO IP
           MOVE 99 TO LOOP-ST

Who Is Excluded

  • The Undocumented
  • Immigrants
  • Those who choose to live off the grid, or who make an intentional choice not to participate in government data collection.
  • Those who experience other obstacles to accessing government systems, including language, disability, poverty, inprisonment, institutional opression.
  • Certain classes of (primarialy public) workers are excluded from the insurance aspect of the program.

Identity Theft

  • In the 1930's, Woolworths sold a wallet which contained a sample Social Security Card. The number printed on it was the actual SSN of a secretary at the wallet company, Ms. Hilda Witcher.
  • Tens of thousands of people adopted Witcher's number as their own over a period of decades. She was eventually issued a new one.
  • Scores of other examples of this nature are known to have occurred.
From 1946-1972, cards carried a written warning that they were not to be used for identification.
  • Regardlerss of intended purpose, SSN's are widely used as unique identifiers across many data systems for government, finance, healthcare, criminal justice, and education.
  • So pervasive that they cannot be easily replaced.
  • Often treated as a password, it's really more like a username.
  • Once compromised, it can generally not be replaced and gives the attacker the ability to impersonate someone with a high level of effectiveness.
  • Massive data breaches involving SSN's occur with relative frequency.
  • 2015 Anthem breach: 78.8 million health insurance customer SSN's exposed
  • 2017 Equifax breach: ~148 million consumers' data leaked, including SSN's
  • 2012-2014 Office of Personell breach: Details of 22 million government employees stolen.
