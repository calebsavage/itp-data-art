Social Insecurity:

What's In a Number?

Background

  • Social Security was created during the great depression. It provides benefits to retirees and people with disabilities.
  • American workers pay into the program with payroll taxes their entire careers, and receive benefits once they reach retirement age.
  • When you think about it, the system is not entirely unlike a ponzi scheme... but that's a topic for a different discussion.

Administration

  • The Social Security Administration is a federal agency which administers the program.
  • This effort requires a massive volume of data to be collected, stored, and analyzed over a period of many decades.
  • This sort of high-volume data collection and processing was enabled by the advent of tabulating machines.
  • It all starts with a number...

The Era of Electromechanical Data Processing

The First Age of Big Data

  • Herman Hollierith's tabulating devices were developed in the 1880s in response to an urgent need to automate the accounting of the 1890 census-- tallying the results from 1880's census took eight years.
  • The earliest examples could do basic counting and sorting operations with data supplied on punched cards.
  • By the mid-1930's, these tabulators could perform simple mathematical operations and work with alphanumeric data.
  • Before the advent of this technology, data processing was bottlenecked by the speed and accuracy of human "computers".
  • Without a means of automatic storage, sorting, and retrieval of information, there was necessarialy more importance placed on personal knowledge and human memory for recordkeeping.
